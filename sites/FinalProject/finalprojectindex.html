Welcome to the project!

This project seeks to pull out the racial bias shown in texts focusing on Roman Emperors of mixed race. This particular group has been chosen because of the differences between interpretations of race in antiquity and modern interpretations of race.The ancient Greeks and Romans were focused less on skin color, and more on the authentic nationality of person. In contrast modern groups, specifically those in the West, have a more complex and often problematic reading of race.

Using the text mining program Voyant, this project seeks to pull the problematic phrases from writing and understand how this impacts today's public. Shown on the site is a search page where specific emperors can be found, or users can browse those that are already included. Each page includes a visualization of the problematic language associated with that person, the citations used, the trends found in the study, and the types of writing included in the citations.

This project is in its beginning phase, and because of this plans to go through multiple iterations. The first change planned is the addition of optical character recognition, because the reference texts can be older, and the inclusion of phrase mining rather than text mining specifically.
